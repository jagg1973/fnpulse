FNPulse is dedicated to providing financial professionals with trustworthy, verified market intelligence. Trust is our most valuable asset, and we maintain it through rigorous adherence to the following standards.

Our Reporting Principles

We believe that financial news must be actionable and accurate. We do not publish rumors as facts. We clearly distinguish between essential news, expert analysis, and opinion pieces.

Every journalist at FNPulse is required to:

  • Prioritize accuracy over speed.
  • Seek comment from subjects of critical reporting.
  • Avoid sensationalism or misleading headlines.
  • Disclose any potential conflicts of interest.

Verification & Sourcing

Primary Sources

We rely on primary sources‚Äîdirect regulatory filings, verified company statements, and on-record interviews‚Äîwhenever possible. When we cite other media reports, we attribute them clearly.

Anonymous Sources

The use of anonymous sources is reserved for situations where:

  1. The information is of vital public or market interest.
  2. The information cannot be obtained any other way.
  3. The source faces accurate risk of retaliation or harm.

When anonymous sources are used, we explain to readers why anonymity was granted (e.g., "officials not authorized to speak publicly"). We strive to corroborate such information with at least one additional source.

Conflicts of Interest

FNPulse editorial staff are prohibited from trading stocks or financial instruments relevant to the specific industries they cover on a short-term basis. Any long-term holdings that could present a perceived conflict must be disclosed to editors and, where relevant, to readers.

Separation of Church and State: Our advertising and commercial teams have no input into editorial decision-making. We do not provide "preview" access of our stories to advertisers or subjects.

Corrections Policy

We are human, and errors can occur. When they do, we fix them promptly and transparently. We do not "stealth edit" material changes.

How We Handle Errors

Factual Errors: We correct the text and append a correction note at the bottom of the article detailing what was changed and the date of the fix.

Clarifications: If a story was factually correct but unclear or misleading, we rewrite for clarity and add a note explaining the update.

Retractions: In rare cases where a story is fundamentally flawed, we will remove the content and replace it with a retraction explanation.

How to Request a Correction

If you believe you have found an error in our reporting, please contact us immediately. Include the article URL, the specific statement you are challenging, and evidence to support your claim.
